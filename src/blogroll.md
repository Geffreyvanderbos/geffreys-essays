Personal sites and blogs I visit regularly.

## [Maggie Appleton](https://maggieappleton.com/)
"Maggie makes visual essays about programming, design, and anthropology." … and she is awesome at it.

## [Dries Buytaert](https://dri.es/about)
Dries is the founder of Drupal and shares insights on (web) technology, entrepreneurship and life.

## [Cory Dransfeldt](https://www.coryd.dev/)
Cory's site is a great exercise in the indieweb philosophy. Love just clicking through the site. Cory has an excellent taste in music, too.

## [John Gruber](https://daringfireball.net/)
Creator of Markdown and author of influential tech blog Daring Fireball. A must read for any technology enthusiast.

## [Jeff Geerling](https://www.jeffgeerling.com/blog)
Sometimes, I just want to nerd out. Jeff's blog is the place to be.

## [Nick Heer](https://pxlnv.com/)
I keep a close eye on Nick Heer his Pixel Envy blog. He talks software, privacy and policies, Apple, and is overall pleasant to read. 

## [Julian Shapiro](https://www.julian.com/)
I often refer to Julian his writing for inspiration and for learning. He has a few in-depth guides and some great blog posts.

## [Derek Sivers](https://sive.rs/)
Derek writes concise essays on life and business, and sharing insights on creativity, simplicity, and independent thinking.

## [Michael Tsai](https://mjtsai.com/)
Michael is much better at keeping tabs on what happens in tech and Apple. I read hist posts to stay informed.

## [Tim Urban](https://waitbutwhy.com/)
On Wait But Why, Tim explains complex topics in a fun and illustrated way.

## [Jason Velazquez](https://www.fromjason.xyz/)
Not does Jason have a great writing style, his website is well designed and personal.

## [Ellana W](https://ellanew.com/)
Ellana writes about future-proofing technology, plain text and overall intentional productivity.

## [Adam Wiggins](https://adamwiggins.com/)
Co‑founder of Ink & Switch, Heroku, and Muse and organizes Local First Conf. Shares interesting thoughts and musings about technology.
